Research Services

In addition to the large selection of technical, operational, and professional training programs available, specific research services are offered to assist employees in supporting their Member, Committee, or other employing office.

Congressional Research Service, Library of Congress

As part of the Library of Congress, and in addition to offering the regular schedule of training programs and educational seminars, the Congressional Research Service (CRS) works exclusively for Members of Congress, Committees, and Congressional staff to assist with nonpartisan analytical research and analysis at all stages of the legislative process. The services offered by CRS include:

  • Online access to CRS products and services through the CRS Web site, http://www.crs.gov/
  • Confidential consultations with CRS experts
  • Written products or confidential memoranda tailored to specific questions requiring information, analysis, or legal interpretation
  • Information, research, and bibliographic services by telephone or in person at conveniently located CRS research centers and the La Follette Congressional Reading Room (locations listed below)
  • Programs including seminars on public policy, budget, and legal issues; institutes on legislative processes; and orientations for Members and staff
