House Parking Program

Parking space allocations for each House office are determined by the Committee on House Administration.  In addition to House offices, employees of the Architect of the Capitol and U.S. Capitol Police are eligible to park in House permit-only areas.

Staff receiving a Metro subsidy are not eligible for parking permits—permanent or temporary.

Additional information on some frequently asked questions concerning parking permits is available at - http://sgtatarms.house.gov/parkingfaq.htm

How to Get a Parking Permit

Employees are authorized for parking permits by their employing office.  When an office parking administrator assigns a permit to an employee, notification of the assignment authorizing the employee and the vehicle to park is submitted on office letterhead, signed by the employing authority (Member or Officer), and sent to the Director, House Garages and Parking Security.  The notification should include the parking permit number from the office’s parking roster; the employee’s name; the make, model, and color of the vehicle; and the license plate information.  There is no charge for a non-reserved parking spot.

Staff wanting to reserve their indoor parking spaces should submit their requests in writing to the House Garages and Parking Security Office and include their current parking assignments and employee identification numbers.  This must be received by the 10th day of the month to be effective for that month’s payroll.  Similarly, to cancel a reserved parking space, House Garages and Parking Security Office must be notified by the 10th day of a month to be effective for that month.
