Security
Internet/Computer Security

The House has developed a comprehensive information systems security program to protect its computer systems and data.  The Security Program includes automated tools that are constantly running on the network to deter hackers and other unauthorized users, as well as policies, procedures, and guidelines that our users follow to keep the network secure.  We recommend that you familiarize yourself with the security policies and guidelines, which can be found on HouseNet (see Information Security in the Technology section).

For an overview of computer security practices at the House, please attend a one-hour Information Security Awareness Seminar offered by the House Learning Center each month.  The course schedule is located on HouseNet (see Training under the Employee Services tab).

SecurIDs

SecurIDs allow users to access the House network from remote locations via dial-up or Virtual Private Network (VPN). Anyone who has a House ID and has permission to access the House network is eligible to have a SecurID.

How to Request a SecurID

  • Fill out a SecurID Request Form, found online on HouseNet (see Information Security in the Technology section) or by calling First Call+ at 202-225-8000—and fax it to the Information Systems Security Office at 202-225-0368.
  • If you work for a Member or Committee, the Member or Chief of Staff must sign the request. If you work for the CAO or any other House officer, the request must be signed by a supervisor.

How to Use a SecurID

  • Enter your User ID.
  • Enter your four-digit PIN followed by the number displayed on the SecurID. Be sure to enter all 10 digits in succession, without entering spaces between numbers.
  • Look at the left of the display window on the SecurID, and be certain that at least one hash mark appears before entering your PIN and the number shown on the token.  Remember that the number changes every 60 seconds.

If your SecurID has expired or is not functioning properly, please call the Information Systems Security Office at
202-226-4988.

Spam Filtering Service

Enterprise Spam Filtering Service is designed to dramatically reduce annoying and potentially offensive unsolicited e-mail (spam) received through the House Messaging System. This opt-in service does not require desktop installation and is available to all House Members, Officers, and staff to control spam addressed to mailboxes on the House Messaging System.  To begin filtering spam, go to “Technology,” and then “E-mail” on HouseNet, and click on the “Printable Getting Started SmartCard” link.  Follow the instructions to start enabling spam filtering.

For more information, call the CAO Technical Support Call Center at 202-225-6002.
