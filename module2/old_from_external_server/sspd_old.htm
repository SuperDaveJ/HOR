Hearing-Impaired Systems in House Hearing Rooms

Several Committee hearing rooms are already equipped with built-in audio induction loop systems for use by individuals who are hearing- impaired. This system may be of benefit to individuals with a “T” switch available on their present hearing aids. In addition, newly renovated Committee hearing rooms have infrared assistive listening systems installed.  Committees are provided with headphones to be used by the public.  For information on the location of the rooms presently equipped with this system, contact the Office of the Architect of the Capitol, Electronics Engineering Division at 202-224-3140.

Accessible Parking at the Capitol and House Office Buildings

Several public parking spaces are provided for individuals with disabilities near or at the accessible entrances. These are available on a first-come, first-served basis. Visitors using these areas must display state-issued handicapped tags or permits. These spaces are for visitors only and not for Congressional staff use. Employees who have special parking needs should contact the House Garages and Parking Security at 5-6749.

Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Services

The Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (ADA) applies to the U.S. House of Representatives through Section 210 of the Congressional Accountability Act of 1995. The Chief Administrative Officer provides ADA Services through its Administrative Counsel. ADA Services can be reached at 202-225-3005 or TTY 202-225-3006, and by requesting the assistance of the Administrative Counsel regarding an ADA issue.

Scope of ADA Services

The general purpose of ADA Services is to enhance awareness of ADA public access issues within House organizations. In that regard, ADA Services provides “one-stop” service to assist House staff in making their facilities and programs accessible to persons with disabilities. ADA Services can provide direction in arranging for sign language interpreters or equipment such as assisted listening devices for temporary needs. ADA Services also provides information on a wide range of other disability matters.
 
ADA Services does not provide legal advice, but works with the employing office and the Office of House Employment Counsel to determine what type of accommodation is advisable in a given situation. Questions concerning an employing office’s obligation under the ADA should be directed to the Office of House Employment Counsel at 202-225-7075.

Audiotapes, Braille, Brochures, Large-Print Publication Services, and Maps

The Office of Publication Services can produce Braille and large-print material for individuals who are blind or have low vision; this includes Member constituency correspondence and tactile maps of the Capitol Building and the Mall. To request these services, please contact Publication Services at 202-225-1908.
 
The House Recording Studio provides video or audiotapes for official purposes at the request of the Member. To request these services, please contact the Recording Studio at 202-225-3941 or call the TTY number at 202-225-1904.

Documents or letters can also be produced and saved in ASCII format on a disc for constituents who use computers equipped with reading capabilities. The Capitol Guide Service and Congressional Special Services Office (CSSO) can quickly walk you through the process of saving files in this format. You can contact CSSO at 202-224-4048.

In addition, brochures and maps are available in several formats for blind or low-vision visitors. The Washington Highlights: Tour Information for Visitors with Disabilities brochure is available in ASCII disc form or audiotape, forwarded upon request from the Congressional Special Services Office (CSSO) at 202-224-4048. The brochure highlights information regarding parking, accessible Washington area tourist sites, TTY phone numbers, and other assisted services.

Besides the CSSO, the Office of Publication Services also distributes the brochures and maps listed above in Braille and large-print formats. To order copies, please contact Publication Services at 202-225-1908.

Audiotapes for Blind or Low-Vision Individuals

The Congressional Special Services Office (CSSO) also offers an Audio Descriptive Tour of the Capitol, for use by blind or low-vision individuals. The tape can be used either before or after taking a tour, and is not intended to be used as a self-guiding tool. Copies of this audiotape are available by contacting CSSO at 202-224-4048.

Sign Language/Interpreting Services

The Congressional Special Services Office (CSSO) has Certified Sign Language Interpreters on staff who can help Congressional offices in communicating with visitors and staff who use sign language. A completed Interpreter Request Form is required to ensure the availability of a Sign Language Interpreter. Contact the CSSO at 202-224-4048 for additional information, for the request forms, and for specific assistance with these requests.

The CSSO does not provide interpreters for Committee meetings, unless requested by a Member to enable a specific witness to communicate with Committee members, and only during that witness’s testimony. Interpretation of a Committee’s meeting for audience members who are hearing-impaired may be arranged by the Committee with a commercial vendor and paid as a Hearing-Related Expense. For assistance in obtaining services from a vendor, please contact the CSSO at 202-224-4048 or ADA Services at 202-225-3005.
 
Sign language interpretation can be provided for official activities, including staff meetings, visits with constituents, press conferences, and work-related instructional classes (such as computer training). Interpretation by the CSSO staff is available only within the Capitol Hill complex.

If you need an interpreter off Capitol Hill, the CSSO can offer assistance in locating appropriate resources. The CSSO maintains a list of interpreter referral agencies nationwide and a listing of individual interpreters who are certified by the National Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf.

The CSSO can also assist Members and staff in locating interpreter services in a Congressional office’s home state for an official function. Please call CSSO at 202-224-4048 for more information. For policy guidance on reimbursement for interpreter services in the District, please contact the Committee on House Administration at 202-225-8281 and refer to the section on “Interpreting and Translating Services” under the Official and Representational Expenses heading of the Members' Congressional Handbook. The Handbook is also available on the Committee’s Web Site at www.house.gov/cha/handbook/welcome.html.

Telecommunications Devices (TTYs) for Individuals Who are Hearing-Impaired

Obtaining a Telecommunications Device (TTY) for Your Office

TTY machines are available for purchase through your office coordinator in B-227 Longworth House Office Building, or by calling 202-225-8000. If your TTY requires a separate telephone line, please call CAO Telecommunications at 202-226-4101.

Using a TTY Device

The Congressional Special Services Office (CSSO) provides seminars on TTY operation and etiquette for staff. To obtain additional information or to register for a seminar, call 202-224-4048.

Availability and Location of Other TTY Devices for Your Use

The House of Representatives Relay Service is available to receive calls for House offices. All incoming calls should be directed to 202-225-1904. Incoming messages will then be delivered to the appropriate office. General access TTYs are available in each House Office Building for use by Members and staff. The locations of the machines are:

  • HB-30 The Capitol (Office of the Chief Administrative Officer)
  • B-227 Longworth House Office Building (CAO First Call+)
  • B-217 Longworth House Office Building (House Office Supply Store)
  • B-310 Rayburn House Office Building (House Recording Studio)
  • 263 Cannon House Office Building (CAO Financial Counseling)
  • H2-217 Ford House Office Building (CAO Immediate Office)

ADA Services operates the Relay Service and is available at 202-225-3005 to answer any questions. The Congressional Special Services Office at 202-224-4048 (CSSO) is also available to answer questions regarding TTY.

Additional Information for TTY Callers

The Congressional Special Services Offices (CSSO) provides referral services to TTY callers requesting information, such as TTY Congressional Directory assistance, and general accessibility for visitors to Washington. Additional phone numbers to contact for assistance are the Congressional Switchboard (TTY) 202-224-3091 and the House Relay Service (TTY) 202-225-1904.

Location of Public Access TTY Devices (Public Pay Phones/TTYs)

Additional TTY devices have been positioned throughout the Capitol complex for use by the public at the following locations:

  • Cannon House Office Building, New Jersey Avenue entrance on the first floor
  • Ford House Office Building, Third Street lobby
  • Longworth House Office Building, Independence Avenue entrance on the first floor, to the left near the elevators
  • Rayburn House Office Building, South Capitol Street entrance on either side of the lobby
  • Capitol Building, North Door, across from the Senate Refectory

Capitol Guide Services & Congressional Special Services Office

The Capitol Guide Service & Congressional Special Services Office (CGS & CSSO) is a joint office under the jurisdiction of the Capitol Guide Board. It provides a variety of services to assist staff, constituents, and visitors. Services include reserved group tours, early morning Congressional tours, dome tours, accessible tours, staff-led tour training classes, publications in alternative formats, amplification devices, sign language interpreting, TTY training and monitoring programs, and classes designed to assist Congressional staff interaction with individuals with disabilities. Additional information or a services brochure is available by contacting the CSSO at 202-224-4048 or TTY 202-224-4049.
