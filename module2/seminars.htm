Chief Administrative Officer (CAO)

The Office of the CAO provides a variety of training courses to House employees, in addition to those available through the House Learning Center:

  • The House Employee Assistance Office offers a variety of custom-tailored workshops for work groups within the House, based upon the particular needs of the office. These courses can address communications in the workplace, team building, conflict management, self-care, dealing with difficult constituents, and more. These programs are provided in addition to the general employee development, performance management, and life-work balance courses that are detailed in the monthly CAO Course Schedule. For more information, contact the employee assistance staff at 202-225-2400 or visit HouseNet and select “Employee Assistance.”
  • CAO Information Systems Security provides several Security Awareness Briefings for employees and systems administrators. To register for the Security Awareness Briefings, please contact the House Learning Center at 202-225-6002, and select Option 3. In addition, a self-paced Security Awareness Training course is available online and is recommended for all House employees. Additional information on these trainings is available at HouseNet under “Information Systems Security, Security Awareness.”
  • CAO Procurement Management provides basic and refresher training for Contracting Officer’s Representatives that includes Statement of Work Preparation Training. Procurement courses are listed in the monthly course calendar.

Office of House Employment Counsel

The House Employment Counsel provides a variety of workplace trainings to assist offices in complying with the employment laws made applicable by the 1995 Congressional Accountability Act. For additional information, contact the Office of House Employment Counsel at 202-225-7075.

Emergency Preparedness Training Program

The Office of Emergency Planning, Preparedness & Operations (OEPPO) offers the House community a number of training programs to support offices and employees in their emergency preparedness and awareness. Training is provided in the following areas:

  • CPR/AED and Basic First Aid Training (monthly)
  • SCape CBRN30 Escape Hood Training (monthly and through online videos available through their Multimedia tab)
  • House Emergency Programs Overview Course (quarterly)
  • Guest Lectures or Special Events (annually)
  • Office Emergency Coordinator (OEC) Roles and Responsibilities (monthly and web-based)
  • Member Office Briefings (as requested)
  • Web-based Training
  • Training for Mobility-impaired Staff and Evacuations

Additional information and a monthly training calendar are available at https://oeppo.house.gov/ or by calling 202-226-0950.

Committee on Standards of Official Conduct

The Committee on Standards of Official Conduct provides briefings for House employees on the Ethics Rules and Ethics Manual, the Financial Disclosure requirements of the House, and the House Gift and Travel rules. Additional information is available at the Committee’s Web site, http://www.house.gov/ethics.

Congressional Research Service, Library of Congress

The Congressional Research Service (CRS) of the Library of Congress provides the Congressional community with a regular and complete schedule of orientations, seminars, and institutes. Many of these courses are designed specifically for newcomers to the Congressional community—for instance, “Congress: An Introduction to Process and Resources,” “Permanent Congressional Staff Orientations,” and “Statutory and Legislative History Research Using Print and Electronic Resources.”

In addition, the CRS provides training for experienced employees—programs such as the “District/State Staff Institute” and the “Advanced Legislative Process Institute.” Additional information and the current listing of trainings are available at http://www.crs.gov/

Online Training

In addition to classroom training, the House Learning Center offers over 90 online, Web-based training classes covering a broad spectrum of Information Technology, business, and professional development courses that are available to both Capitol Hill and District employees. All of these courses can be taken at any time, and they do not require that you complete the entire course. You can jump to specific topics that suit your needs and complete only those sections of the training course that interest you. In addition, a Multimedia Library is available from which to request CDs and videos of other training programs.
