The Office of Attending Physician (OAP) provides acute/urgent care for all who work on or visit Capitol Hill. This includes Members of Congress, the Justices of the Supreme Court, Congressional staff, other employees and support staff, the Pages, the United States Capitol Police, and all visitors to our Nation’s Capitol.

In addition to providing care for a wide range of patients, the OAP also provides an entire spectrum of services, from emergency care to travel clinics to occupational exams and beyond.

The following is a basic listing of the medical services available to Congressional staff and employees:

  • Emergency evaluation for injuries and serious illnesses
  • Free flu vaccinations
  • Administration of allergy shots
  • Physician referrals
  • Travel advice and screenings
  • Occupational exams and certifications

If you have any questions regarding your eligibility for medical services provided by the Office of Attending Physician, or to inquire about a particular service, please call 202-225-5421.

Allergy Immunization Program

Anyone with a Congressional Staff ID is eligible to take advantage of our allergy immunization program. This service is a valuable resource dedicated to keeping our workforce healthy and on the job. For specific information on how to enroll, please call 5-5421.

Environmental Health

The Environmental and Occupational Health Section of the Office of Attending Physician manages programs which evaluate and control factors that may impact the health of those who work in or visit the U.S. Capitol and Legislative Office Buildings .

Nursing Mothers Program

Every Health Unit now has a private area for nursing mothers.

For Medical Emergencies—CALL 911

In the event of any medical emergency—whether involving a Member, employee, or visitor—the Attending Physician’s response team will respond and provide emergency care until the patient can be transported to the appropriate medical facility.

**IMPORTANT NOTE:  Calling 911 from a “Land line” phone connects with USCP and activates the OAP emergency response.  Calling 911 from a cell phone will connect with DC Fire/EMS.
