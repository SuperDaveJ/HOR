Debitek Card

Don’t want to hassle with cash? Use Debitek, the House-based debit card that can be used for any food purchase at all dining service locations listed below (except for the Ford Cafeteria and Ford Carryout), and with all vending machines.

Purchase a new Debitek Card for $5 at any of the “cash-to-card” machines located in most House dining facilities. You can add up to $150 to your card, but remember that cards are non-transferable. So if you lose your card, you will lose any money you put on the card.

If you have questions regarding the Debitek Card program, please contact First Call+ at 5-8000.  You can access FAQs about the Debitek Card by visiting HouseNet and selecting “Services”; then select “Dining Services & Menus.”

Fitness: Gold’s Gym

The House has partnered with Gold’s Gym to provide House staff with affordable and convenient access to many Gold’s Gym fitness facilities throughout the Washington, DC, metro area. As part of this offering, benefits-eligible House employees may take advantage of the following Gold’s Gym membership package:

  • A $25 onetime enrollment fee and a monthly membership fee of $13.95 for use of the Capitol Hill facility exclusively, or $15.95 for use of numerous Gold’s Gyms in the DC, Virginia, and Maryland area
  • An initial complimentary session with a fitness trainer, and discounted packages for additional sessions
  • “Travel” passes good for limited use at all 619 franchise gyms worldwide

Interested staff can sign up at the Gold’s Capitol Hill Gym by presenting their House ID and their employee number from their House payroll stub along with any initial payment required.

If you have questions regarding the House Gold’s Gym program, please contact Gold’s Gym at 202-554-GOLD.

Postal Operations

House Postal Operations handles the delivery and collection of mail and packages to all House offices, and accepts and delivers FedEx, UPS, and DHL packages and mail. The Postal Operations Call Center phone number is 301-336-8820.

Mail Collection and Delivery Schedule

Monday–Friday deliveries:  

 7:30 a.m., 10:30 a.m., and 2:30 p.m.
Monday–Friday collections: 11:00 a.m., 3:00 p.m., and 5:00 p.m.
Saturday delivery; collection: 10:30 a.m.

Inter-office Mail

Blue envelopes are designed for inter-office official mail within the House complex. If you would like to order more blue envelopes, please fill out and submit a blue envelope order form, found on HouseNet by going to “Services,” and then to “Mail.”

Mail Safety

The House has instituted security measures within our mail delivery system to ensure the safety of Members, staff, and visitors. All deliveries are inspected for chemical, biological, radiological, and explosive materials. While most of these security measures are transparent to your offices, some do impact the mail and the delivery schedule, and affect the manner in which offices receive incoming letters and packages.

House Postal Operations processes all incoming and outgoing U.S. Postal Service mail for House offices, as well as all incoming mail and packages from private shippers (UPS, FedEx, and DHL).

Letters

All First-Class letters destined for House offices in Washington, DC are irradiated by the U. S. Postal Service prior to delivery to the House; this mail is irradiated to remove possible biological contaminants. The average time for the delivery of an item to its destination is five days from the postmark date.  It is recommended that Members promote other communication mechanisms, such as e-mail or fax, for their constituents to reach them on time-sensitive matters. While the secure mail process that your mail will undergo may cause some inconvenience for you and constituents, it is designed to ensure that mail delivered to the House is safe for you to open.

Packages

Packages addressed to House offices are not irradiated before delivery to the House.  All packages from the U.S. Postal Service and private carriers (FedEx, UPS, etc.), are delivered to an off-site processing center.  Each package is tested externally, opened, tested internally, photographed, logged, and placed in quarantine.

Once a letter or package arrives and begins the secure mail process, it cannot be retrieved until the testing and quarantine periods are concluded and the item is released for delivery. This testing and quarantine process adds an average of two business days to the normal delivery process for a package. 

Courier Deliveries

The Congressional Acceptance Site (CAS) is located on 2nd and D Streets NE (Lot 16 across from the Capitol Police Headquarters).  The CAS will accept items from local couriers with proper identification. These couriered packages can be delivered in three hours after receipt using an expedited testing process.

Inside Mail

Inside Mail is a delivery service for inter-office communications on the Capitol campus. This service is provided to support the conduct of the official business of Members, Committees, Officers of the House, and to legislative, administrative, and other staff organizations within the Congress. Blue inter-office envelopes are provided free to House offices for use with Inside Mail. There are three deliveries and three daily collections of Inside Mail from House offices Monday through Friday, and one collection and delivery on Saturday.

Any mailbox that you use for the collection of mail from your office must be kept inside your office, not out in the hallway.  A mailbox that is in the hallway will not have mail collected from it, and the mailbox will be removed for security reasons.
