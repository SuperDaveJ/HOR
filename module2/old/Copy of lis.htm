 Tracking Legislation, House Floor Proceedings, and the House Schedule

Each year Congress studies and considers hundreds of pieces of new legislation.  With so many bills pending, how do you find out about the legislation that interests you and your Member?  Here are some links to help you track legislation, House floor proceedings, and the House schedule.

House Floor Proceedings
A detailed schedule of House committee meetings for the current legislative day:               http://clerk.house.gov/floorsummary/floor.html

This Week on the House Floor
Schedule of legislation the House intends to consider this week:
        http://www.house.gov/house/floor/thisweek.htm

Daily Committee Schedules
A detailed schedule of House committee meetings for the current legislative day:
        http://www.house.gov/daily/comlist.html

Daily House Calendars
Daily calendars of the U.S. House of Representatives and the history of legislation:
        http://www.gpoaccess.gov/calendars/house/index.html

Legislative Year Calendar
Calendar of session days and district work periods:
        http://majorityleader.house.gov/docUploads/2007-CALENDAR.pdf

Bill Summary and Status
A detailed log of House floor proceedings for the current legislative day:
        http://thomas.loc.gov/bss/d109query.html

Legislative Bell System

The House Legislative Electric Bell Signals

Hall clocks and wall fixtures in the House office buildings will light up with the respective number of bell rings listed below. One light remains lit in the House office buildings while the House is in session. Listed below is an explanation of the Legislative Bell System:

  • 1 long bell, pause, followed by 3 bells—Signals the start or continuation of a notice quorum call. (This call will be terminated if and when 100 Members appear.)
  • 1 long bell—Signals termination of a notice quorum call
  • 2 bells—Signals a 15-minute recorded vote
  • 2 bells, pause, followed by 2 bells—Signals a manual roll call vote (the bells ring again when the Clerk reaches the Rs)
  • 2 bells, pause, followed by 5 bells—Signals a first vote in a series of votes. (The first vote will be a minimum of 15 minutes with successive votes of not less than 5 minutes each. Each successive vote signaled by 5 bells.)
  • 3 bells—Signals a quorum call (either initially or after a notice quorum has been converted to a regular quorum call. Members have a minimum of 15 minutes to be recorded.)
  • 3 bells, pause, followed by 3 bells—Signals a manual quorum call (the bells ring again when the Clerk reaches the Rs)
  • 3 bells, pause, followed by 5 bells—Signals a quorum call in Committee of the Whole, which may be immediately followed by a 5-minute recorded vote.
  • 4 bells— Signals adjournment of the House
  • 5 bells— Signals a 5-minute recorded vote
  • 6 bells—Signals recess of the House
  • 12 bells rung at 2-second intervals—Signals Civil Defense Warning
