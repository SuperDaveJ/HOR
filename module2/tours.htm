Tours of the Capitol

The United States Capitol is open to the public for guided tours Monday through Saturday, from 9:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.

Free tickets for Capitol tours are available on a first-come, first-served basis at the Capitol Guide Service kiosk, located on the southwest side of the Capitol near the intersection of First Street SW, and Independence Avenue. Ticket distribution begins at 9:00 a.m. daily.

The Congressional Special Services Office (CSSO) provides information about tours for persons with specific needs. You may reach them by telephone at 202-224-4048 (voice) or 202-224-4049 (TDD).
If you have any questions, contact the Capitol Guide Service at 202-225-6827.

Tours of the House Floor

The House Floor is open for staff-led tours from 9:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday through Friday, only when the House is out of session. Tours can be conducted only by a permanent Congressional staff person with a valid tour ID card. One House Floor tour ID card is available for each Congressional office.

For more information and to access FAQs, visit the Sergeant at Arms’ Web Site at http://sgtatarms.house.gov and select “Visiting the Capitol.” If you have any questions, call 202-224-3235. (Adapted from the Sergeant at Arms’ Web Site.)

Tours of the Capitol Dome

Capitol Dome tours are available to House Members and Officers and are conducted by a member of the Capitol Guide Service. Tours are available Monday through Friday, from 9:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. Tours last between 45 and 60 minutes. To request a tour, call the Capitol Guide Service tour scheduling office at 202-224-8406. Requests may be made up to eight weeks in advance.

The group size of a Dome Tour is limited to the Capitol Guide, the Member, and a maximum of seven guests. On the day of the tour, the Member should bring the tour group to the Crypt of the Capitol and meet the tour guide at the Capitol Guide Service/Congressional Special Services Office, which is located in the Crypt, Room S-156

Capitol Guide Services & Congressional Special Services Office

The Capitol Guide Service & Congressional Special Services Office (CGS & CSSO) is a joint office under the jurisdiction of the Capitol Guide Board. It provides a variety of services to assist staff, constituents and visitors. Services include reserved group tours, Congressional (early morning) tours, dome tours, accessible tours, staff-led tour training classes, publications in alternative formats, amplification devices, sign language interpreting, TTY training and monitoring programs, and classes designed to assist Congressional staff interaction with individuals with disabilities. Additional information or a services brochure is available by contacting the CSSO at 202-224-4048 or TTY 202-224-4049.

Flags

As an employee of the U.S. House of Representatives, you can have a flag flown over the Capitol for presentation purposes.  Flags and certificates can be purchased from the House Supply Store, B-217 Longworth House Office Building. Flags and certificates can be taken to the Architect’s Flag Office, Room HT-16, The Capitol, with a letter showing the names of the individuals for whom particular flags are to be flown.  Certificates will be prepared confirming that the flags have flown over the Capitol, and you will be notified when they are ready for pickup.
