ID Badges
Location: 321 Cannon House Office Building
Phone:    202-225-3820
Fax:    202-225-0239
Hours of Operation:  9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday
Web Site:  http://sgtatarms.house.gov

The Office of Identification Services provides identification cards to permanent and temporary House of Representatives and Architect of the Capitol (AOC) staff, interns, pages, fellows, detailees, as well as the Members and the immediate family and spouses of Members of Congress.  Vendors and contractors sponsored by a House or AOC office are also issued identification badges. In addition, ID Services issues liaison identification badges to paid employees of the Executive Branch and other Congressional support agencies who have a specific, frequent business need to access the Capitol campus outside of public building hours. House identification badges are the property of the House and must be returned when employment with the House ends. Additional information on some frequently asked questions concerning ID badges is available at http://sgtatarms.house.gov/idservicesfaq.htm.

Initial ID Badge

To obtain an ID card, new staff must follow this process:

  • Fill out a 110th Congress ID request form.
  • Obtain the signature of the employing Member (Officer) on the ID request form.
  • Submit the form to ID Services in person or by fax to 202-225-0239.  Forms should be submitted at least twenty-four (24) hours before an ID is required.  This enables data entry to be performed before the requesting staffer arrives at the ID Offices, and thereby reduces waiting time.
  • Presentation of proper photo identification, which includes a valid driver’s license, non-driver’s ID card, domestic or foreign passport, or U.S. military card, will be required before an ID badge is issued.

Lost Identification Cards

Lost ID cards must be reported to ID services, either by telephone or in person.  The reports should include the cardholder’s name, Social Security number, and fax number, so that an affidavit for a lost ID may be completed.  Both the Member and cardholder MUST sign the affidavit.  There is a waiting period of three (3) business days from the time the ID card is reported to ID Services as lost or stolen, before an ID will be reissued.

Temporary Identification Stickers

Permanent staff may get a temporary ID by reporting to the Capitol Police House Division Detail Office, located in B-220 Longworth House Office Building.  Please be prepared to present photo identification in order to receive a temporary ID.  In addition, your employment status will be verified prior to issuance of the temporary ID.
