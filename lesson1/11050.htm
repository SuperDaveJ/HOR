Before the Constitution
The Great Compromise

Eventually, the Convention reached a compromise on the structure of Congress (known now as the Great Compromise). Congress was to be a bicameral body, with:

  • One house of Congress (the House of Representatives) providing representation proportional to each state's population, thus creating a "house of the people" that would represent public opinion

  • The other house of Congress (the Senate) providing equal representation, thus creating a house that represents the governments of the individual states, which would be less susceptible to variations of mass sentiment
