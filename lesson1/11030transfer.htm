Before the Constitution
The Constitutional Convention

As time went on, the Articles of Confederation proved inadequate for meeting the needs of our government, not only with regards to the structure of Congress, but with other issues as well. So, the Continental Congress convened the Constitutional Convention (known then as the Philadelphia Convention) in Philadelphia in May of 1787 to revise the Articles.
