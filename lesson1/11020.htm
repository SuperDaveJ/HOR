Before the Constitution
Articles of Confederation

In a nutshell, the U.S. House of Representatives was created as a way to represent public opinion in Congress....

Before the Constitution, our government was operating under the Articles of Confederation, and Congress was a unicameral (single-chamber) body in which each state held one vote. However, several members of Congress felt that one vote was not enough to adequately represent the people from states with large populations, and they wanted proportional representation based on a state's population.
