After the Constitution
The Growth of of a House

The first House of Representatives convened on April 1, 1789, with 65 representatives.

Today, the House has 435 representatives, a number currently fixed by the Reapportionment Act of 1929.

In addition to the 435 representatives, House membership also includes four delegates from American Samoa, the District of Columbia, Guam, and the U.S. Virgin Islands, and one resident commissioner from Puerto Rico.

Did you know that in 1959, House membership reached an all-time high of 437 representatives—two representatives more than the Reapportionment Act of 1929 allowed?

When Alaska and then Hawaii joined the Union in 1959, the law was changed to allow the total membership of the House of Representatives to increase to 436 and then to 437, apportioning one new Representative for each of those states. The total returned to 435 in 1963, when the 1960 census apportionment took effect.
