Before the Constitution
How to Structure Congress

One of the many issues discussed during the Convention was how Congress was to be structured, including how many representatives in Congress would be allotted each state.

Some delegates—mostly those from larger states—wanted a bicameral (two-chamber) body, with the lower house providing proportional representation based on a state's population, and the upper house elected by the lower house.

Other delegates—typically those from smaller states, which would be at a disadvantage with proportional representation—wanted to maintain the unicameral body and provide equal representation to all states, regardless of population.
