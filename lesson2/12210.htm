United States Capitol Police

The United States Capitol Police (USCP) was created by Congress in 1828, with the sole mission of providing security for the United States Capitol building.  Almost two centuries later, the USCP has expanded in numbers, duties, and responsibilities.

Today, the USCP's primary responsibilities are to protect life and property; prevent, detect, and investigate criminal acts; and enforce traffic regulations throughout a large complex of Congressional buildings, parks, and thoroughfares. Additionally, they are responsible for protecting Members of Congress, the Officers of the United States Senate and the U.S. House of Representatives, and their families throughout the entire United States, its territories and possessions, and throughout the District of Columbia.
