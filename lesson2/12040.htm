House Leadership
Majority and Minority Whips

As the third-ranking Members of the House leadership and elected by their party caucus or conference, both the Majority and Minority Whips assist their parties' leadership in managing their legislative program on the House Floor. The Whips keep track of all legislation and ensure that their party members are present when important measures are to be voted upon.

The Whips also distribute the "Weekly Whip" and the "Daily Whip," which detail by week and day, respectively, the legislation on the House Floor. In addition to the “Weekly Whip” and the “Daily Whip,” the Whips also distribute the House calendar, which lists the days of the year on which the House of Representatives is expected to be in session.

The Majority Whip helps to coordinate scheduling and strategy with the Speaker of the House and the House Majority Leader.

The Minority Whip is involved in planning opposition to the majority.

 

 

The term Whip is derived from the British term "whipper-in," who was the person responsible for keeping the hounds from leaving the pack during a fox hunt.

Whip was first adopted as a political term in the British Parliament around 1836. The term was first used in the U.S. House of Representatives in 1897, when James A. Tawney (R-MN) was appointed Whip by Speaker Thomas Reed (R-ME) to help him keep track of party members.
